About Census 2027

India's 16th National Census — First Digital Census

Census 2027 is India's 16th National Census and the country's first fully digital population enumeration exercise. It is being conducted in two phases — Phase 1 is House Listing Operations from April to September 2026, and Phase 2 is Population Enumeration in February 2027.

The census will cover 36 States and Union Territories, over 7,000 sub-districts, 5,128 statutory towns, and nearly 6.4 lakh villages. Over 30 lakh enumerators are participating in data collection using mobile applications. For the first time, citizens can self-enumerate through a secure online portal in 16 languages. The government has approved an outlay of ₹11,718.24 crore for this exercise.

Self-Enumeration Portal

Fill your census details online before the enumerator visits

The Self-Enumeration facility allows households to fill in their census details online through a secure portal. You can log in using your registered mobile number, submit your family's information, and generate a unique Self-Enumeration ID (QR code).

When the enumerator visits, they will scan this QR code to confirm your household is done — saving significant time for both you and the census team. Self-enumeration windows are open for 15 days in each state before the official house listing begins.

The portal is available in 16 languages including Hindi, English, Gujarati, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, and more.

State Census Offices

Directorate of Census Operations — State-wise contacts

Each state and union territory has a Directorate of Census Operations headed by a Director of Census Operations (DCO). These offices coordinate all census activities within their jurisdiction.

Key state offices include:
• Gujarat — DCO Gandhinagar
• Maharashtra — DCO Mumbai
• Uttar Pradesh — DCO Lucknow
• Bihar — DCO Patna
• West Bengal — DCO Kolkata
• Tamil Nadu — DCO Chennai
• Kerala — DCO Thiruvananthapuram

For state-specific queries, contact your respective DCO office or visit censusindia.gov.in/state-offices
